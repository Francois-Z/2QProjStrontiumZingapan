Allomancy

Allomancy is an end-positive art, where an allomancer gets power from an external source. Here, Preservation itself fuels allomancy. Allomancers "burn" metal in their stomach to trigger an effect, which depends on what metal is being burned. Note, however, metals are not necessarily Investiture; metals "connect" to Preservation's Investiture.

An allomancer can burn either only one type of metal or all metals. The former we call a Misting and the latter a Mistborn. An allomancer can enhance the effects from a metal if they "flare" it, consequently exhausting it faster.

Feruchemy

Feruchemy has long existed before the Catacendre and even the rule of the Lord Ruler. Feruchemy is an end-neutral art formed by both Preservation and Ruin. A feruchemist cannot lose or gain power practicing this art; they store power, therefore changing the time they use this power. A feruchemist can either only store power in one type of metal or all of it. The former we call a Ferring and the latter a Full Feruchemist.

They store this Investiture by investing or "filling" a metalmind, then accessing or "tapping" it for later. Unlike an allomancer who gets a fixed amount of power, a feruchemist can tap more power by exhausting the reserve quicker. This means that they are not limited to fixed amounts of power and therefore can overpower an allomancer.

Hemalurgy

Hemalurgy is a fairly complicated art. First, it is end-negative as it usually loses power because of the law of Hemalurgic Decay. It states that a hemalurgic spike loses power if it is outside a body, but one can "trick" the spike by setting it inside blood or a slab of meat.

This brings me to the second feature: Hemalurgy can steal powers or characteristics from an individual using metallic spikes, which we call a hemalurgic spike. To do this, one must first pierce the victim with a metallic spike, often killing them, then pierce it once more in someone else to grant them the power.
